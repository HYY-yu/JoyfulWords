One of the highest-leverage moves in content production is repurposing.

A strong blog post already contains your argument, evidence, and structure. Turning it into a presentation should be a transformation step, not a second project.

## Step 1: Prepare the Article for Slide Logic

Before conversion, check whether your article already has clear sections:

- context/problem
- core framework
- practical examples
- summary and next actions

If sections are clear, AI can map them into a slide narrative with much less noise.

## Step 2: Decide Presentation Outcome First

Choose the target scenario before generating slides:

- team sync
- client briefing
- public talk
- internal training

The same article can produce very different decks depending on audience and time limit.

## Step 3: Extract Slide-Level Messages

For each major section, define one message sentence.

This avoids the most common failure in AI-generated decks: too much text copied directly from article paragraphs.

A good slide usually has:

- one clear claim
- two to three supporting bullets
- one visual cue or data point

## Step 4: Generate Deck Draft and Keep It Editable

When AI generates your first deck, ensure the output remains editable in standard presentation software.

Editable structure matters because:

- you can adjust hierarchy quickly
- you can tailor language to audience context
- you can improve visual pacing before delivery

A slide image stack might look polished, but it blocks real iteration.

## Step 5: Add Reuse Components

Before final export, add:

- opening context slide
- section transition slides
- closing CTA slide

These reusable components speed up future deck creation and keep brand consistency.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Converting before article structure is stable
- Keeping article-level detail in every slide
- Treating AI output as final without human review
- Losing source references during conversion
